About Me

I'm a Doctoral Researcher at TU Wien's Atominstitut and a Vienna Center for Quantum Science and Technology (VCQ) Fellow specializing in Casimir force metrology and quantum vacuum interactions.

My research focuses on developing ultra-precise measurement techniques to explore fundamental quantum forces and their technological applications. I work at the intersection of quantum physics, precision metrology, and advanced instrumentation.

With extensive experience in laser systems, interferometry, and nanoscale sensing, I'm passionate about translating fundamental research into innovative solutions.

Research & Projects

CANNEX Project

Developing state-of-the-art Casimir force metrology using plane-parallel plate geometry, with sub-nanometer resolution and precision environmental control.

Seismic Isolation Systems

Designed six-axis isolation systems using GAS filters and inverted pendulums for ultra-sensitive quantum measurements.

Thermal Control Systems

Created active thermal shielding with radiative blackbody control achieving temperature stability below 0.3 mK.

Advanced Laser Systems

Built tunable fiber lasers and high-power SHG systems for spectroscopy and precision measurements.
